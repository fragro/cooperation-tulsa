Our Mission
At Cooperation Tulsa, our mission is to empower and uplift our community through collective and inclusive practices. Based in Tulsa, we strive to build a more equitable and resilient city by fostering local food sovereignty, expanding communal access to land, and promoting direct democracy. Embracing principles of mutual aid and collective action, we are dedicated to creating a network of community gardens and local councils that not only nourish our neighborhoods but also strengthen our shared commitment to social justice and sustainable living.

FLAT ROCK URBAN GARDEN
Flat Rock is a plot of 2.5 acres in north Tulsa, donated by Mary Odom in 2021 after seeing our work on the Vernon AME Community Garden. When we received this land, it was being used as a dump site and had huge piles of discarded junk. However, that’s in the rearview mirror now! We have since cleaned up all the most major dump areas on the property.

These days we are now in the process of not only restoring the native ecology, but we have built above-ground garden beds and have established several lanes in-ground, remediating the soil as we go. We are also developing a composting operation, which we first hope to use on our own crops, but then, as we produce more, to sell to gardens in our community.

We plan to take the food grown at Flat Rock and distribute it to the community directly. We also hope to involve the community in making decisions about what we will grow in the future!

Location: 5481 N. Peoria Ave

Time: Every Saturday from around 1pm until sunset

How you can participate: Stop by on any work day. All skill and ability levels welcome!

Organizer education
The goal of the Cooperation Tulsa Organizer Education Program is to bring together local organizers in order to study important works in theory, history, and praxis. In these meetings, participants come together and read the text aloud, taking breaks to discuss insights within the work. We find that this slower approach works best because it gives us time to think through the pieces we are reading and discuss points of interest.

Location: Online

Time: 8:00pm-9:30pm

2nd and 4th Monday of every month

How you can participate: Discord link

Church of the restoration community garden
In the latter half of 2021, Cooperation Tulsa secured a grant from TYPROS in order to construct a community garden at Church of the Restoration. This garden was built as a collaborative project along with the surrounding community and the church’s congregation. 

The intention of this garden is to increase the food sovereignty of the Greenwood district and to empower those within the surrounding community to make democratic decisions about what they will grow and consume. 

Location: Church of the Restoration,

1314 N Greenwood Ave, Tulsa, OK 74106

Status: Ongoing, managed autonomously by the church and community

How you can participate: Reach out to Church of the Restoration!
Vernon AME community garden
In 2020, Cooperation Tulsa crowdfunded enough money to build its first community garden in the Greenwood district at the historic Vernon AME Church. This community garden was constructed as a collaborative project between Cooperation Tulsa and the church congregation. 

Location: Vernon AME Church,

311 N Greenwood Ave, Tulsa, OK 74120

Status: Ongoing, managed autonomously by the church and community

How you can participate: Reach out to Vernon AME!
Cooperation Tulsa community center
In January 2022, Cooperation Tulsa opened a community center. Our aim with the community center was to create a venue to host teach-ins, free stores, dinners, lectures, and other events. But, even though it was highly successful, it was not necessarily financially sustainable. Instead, we redirected our efforts into Flat Rock.
